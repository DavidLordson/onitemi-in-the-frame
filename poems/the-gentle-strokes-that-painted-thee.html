The Gentle Strokes that painted thee

Woman in the frame
Envy of Mona Lisa
Let my fingers take in
The tapestry of your greatness

I sit at your empty golden plaque
Words unable to quantify
What your maker put in you

I see men come and go
They adore the woman
Secured behind glass
They write words on the plaque
Your hands step out of the frame
To wipe off their assumptions
Feeble attempts to capture
The beauty of my beloved

You return to your smile
Waiting patiently
As I take a tulip stalk
From its five brethren
With gentle strokes to paint thee
