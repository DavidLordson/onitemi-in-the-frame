Pink Grass, Blue Boy

He comes from a field of grey, used to opalescent leaves and iridescent fruits
She comes with blue skies and white lilies
Flowers in her hands, the sun in her hair
He spots her by the river
The first time sunrise ever felt inviting to him

She looked in his eyes with curiosity
Wondering how he lived in his fields of grey
Below him the river bellowed
Rushing and crashing into rocks
She held out her hand and with a smile
Beckoned him over

He was scared, as the rivers whispered
Thoughts of rejection and replayed scenes of betrayal
Yet, as he put his first foot in the water
his little staff grew blue

She put her foot in the water, and the grass softened her step
Pink, soft, wafting in the waters, that had stilled
He reached for her hand, and the blue grew, covering his hands, his chest, his head.
Until he was blue

She showed him what lay beyond the river
A form of femininity he had never known before
The pink grass embraced him, as her sun floated from her hair to the sky, he sat on the grass.
To study, to admire, to finally feel
